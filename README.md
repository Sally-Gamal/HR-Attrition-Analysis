## 2. 📝 Project Overview
This project analyzes HR data to understand the factors influencing employee attrition. By identifying patterns in departments, job roles, and employee satisfaction, the goal is to provide actionable insights to reduce turnover and improve retention.

---

## 3. 🔍 Key Questions Answered
* Which department has the highest turnover rate?
* Does salary level (Monthly Income) impact the decision to leave?
* How does work-life balance (Overtime & Business Travel) affect attrition?
* Is there a correlation between Job Satisfaction and employee retention?

---

## 💡 4. Key Insights (Conclusion)
* **Department Impact:** The **R&D Department** has the highest number of employees, but the **Sales Department** also shows significant turnover.
* **Travel Stress:** Employees who **Travel Frequently** represent **52%** of the attrition, suggesting that heavy travel is a major factor in leaving.
* **Overtime Factor:** **28%** of employees who left were working **Overtime**, indicating potential burnout.
* **Salary Gap:** There is a huge gap in income; Managers and Research Directors earn over **$16k**, while Sales Representatives earn around **$2.6k**, which may explain high turnover in entry-level roles.
* **Education Field:** Employees from **Life Sciences** and **Medical** backgrounds are the most common in the company and also show the most attrition.
* **Tenure:** Attrition is highest among employees who have been with the company for **6-10 years**, suggesting a need for mid-career engagement programs.
